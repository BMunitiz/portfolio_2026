Spanish Unemployment Analysis

This comprehensive analysis project explores Spanish unemployment data across regions and time periods, providing insights into regional disparities and temporal trends.


Project Overview

The analysis focuses on understanding the Spanish unemployment landscape by examining data across regions and time periods. The project leverages official government data, ETL processes and data analysis techniques to uncover meaningful patterns in regional, gender, age and sector disparities and temporal trends.


Data Source

The dataset contains information about Spanish unemployment data with details including:

  • Regional unemployment rates across Spain
  • Gender and age group breakdowns
  • Sectoral employment data
  • Temporal trends over multiple years

Key Findings

Analysis reveals significant variations in unemployment rates across different regions and demographic groups:

  • South regions of Spain show consistently higher unemployment rates compared to northern regions
  • Women over 45 show higher unemployment rates than any other demographic group
  • Similar trends in both genders, consistently reducing the difference over time
  • Service sector has the strongest pull in unemployment rates

Visual Insights

Key visualizations from the analysis include:

Unemployment by Region: Bar chart showing unemployment rates across Spanish regions

Gender and Age Group Comparison: Bar chart comparing unemployment rates by gender and age group

Unemployment Trends: Line graph showing unemployment changes over time


Technical Approach

The analysis uses:

  • Python and Pandas for data extraction and manipulation
  • Statistical analysis for unemployment comparisons
  • Grouping and aggregation techniques
  • Visualizations with Plotly and Folium

Insights

This analysis provides valuable insights for:

  • Policy makers understanding regional disparities
  • Academic researchers studying labor market trends
  • Employment agencies targeting specific demographics
  • Desired employment outcomes for job seekers
